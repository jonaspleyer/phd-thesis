\section{Additional Details to elementary \acsp{ca}}
\label{supplement:additional-details-for-ca}
Subsection~\ref{subsection:elemntary-ca} discussed the relation of \acp{ca} to \acp{cabm} in which
Figure~\ref{fig:ca-filtered-single} showed results of various \acp{ca} which were obtained by
applying the respective rules to the same initial condition of only seeding with a single living
cell.
Here we present additional results for the same rules, underlining the previously made claim that
the produced patterns are somewhat robust with respect to varying initial conditions.

However, we only investigate this exemplary by studying the three additional cases:
Figure~\ref{fig:ca-filtered-double1} two living cells spaced apart by 20 cell units, then two
living cells which are only separated by a single unoccupied cell (see
Figure~\ref{fig:ca-filtered-double1-close}) and finally when seeding with alternating occupancies
(living/dead) in a finite subregion (see Figure~\ref{fig:ca-filtered-alternating}).
In the last figure, we have purposefully excluded rules 032, 104, 160, 164 and 232 which decay
linearly and ultimately cease to exist after $\approx n/4$ steps where $n$ is the number of
initially placed living cells.

\begin{figure}[H]
    \includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figures/ca-rules/double1.pdf}
    \caption[Elementary \acsp{ca} seeded with two cells spaced apart]{
        \acp{ca} as presented in Figure~\ref{fig:ca-filtered-single}, seeded with
        two living cells, separated by 20 unit cells.
    }
    \label{fig:ca-filtered-double1}
\end{figure}

\begin{figure}[H]
    \includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figures/ca-rules/double1-close.pdf}
    \caption[Elementary \acsp{ca} seeded with two cells close to each other]{
        \acp{ca} as presented in Figure~\ref{fig:ca-filtered-single}, seeded with
        two living cells, separated only by a single unoccupied cell.
    }
    \label{fig:ca-filtered-double1-close}
\end{figure}

\begin{figure}[H]
    \includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figures/ca-rules/alternating.pdf}
    \caption[Elementary \acsp{ca} seeded alternatingly]{
        \acp{ca} as presented in Figure~\ref{fig:ca-filtered-single}, seeded alternatingly
        with living/unoccupied cells in a confined subregion.
    }
    \label{fig:ca-filtered-alternating}
\end{figure}

The results presented in Figures~\ref{fig:ca-filtered-double1}, \ref{fig:ca-filtered-double1-close},
\ref{fig:ca-filtered-alternating} show that the same patterns emerge compared to the case of seeding
with only a single living cell (see Figure~\ref{fig:ca-filtered-single}).
Therefore they underline the previously made claim that the formation of these patterns are somewhat
robust with respect to the initial conditions.
